by HUW WARMENHOVEN,Canberra, Australia

We currently are in the midst of an era of change, not just a change of era. This distinct moment in history calls for the next generation of our Church to see themselves, in the words of Pope Francis, as the ‘now of God.’ But, how?

A paradigm shift can be felt rippling throughout our Church. Young people are not responding in the same way to the proclamation of the Gospel. Why? I believe it is not ‘what’ but ‘how’ the timeless message is being proclaimed in our time. Youth and young adults desire the truth. They want to journey with Christ and be accompanied by His Church as they seek to answer three fundamental questions: Where do I belong? What do I believe? Who do I want to become?

These are the questions that have led to a new series to be released with Shalom World: Seekers. ‘Seekers’ is a 15-part series that explores topics of faith, life and meaning. It essentially asks the question: what are you seeking and how can you encounter Christ on this journey? In answering this simple question, it is our hope that all can come to know they have been created out of love, for a life of love, by a God of love. Seekers explores relevant and relatable topics like marriage, family and work, showcasing the power of story in the form of fireside chats and interviews combined with prayer and worship that elicits a call to action in our daily lives. This experience of sharing stories has been pivotal to my own faith journey. Testimony of the Holy Spirit at work has been a constant source of inspiration; from my initial opportunities to explore a personal relationship with Jesus in my teenage years to the essential focus of my experience of mission in different ministry contexts across Australia, Africa, Asia and Europe.

One of these ministry contexts has been my work in the Archdiocese of Canberra & Goulburn, Australia. It was through my role in leading youth ministry in the Archdiocese that I came to encounter Shalom Media at the Australian Catholic Youth Festival 2019. Hearing their story and passion to share the gospel today and let the world know that Christ loves them as they are, has inspired me to seek to proclaim the Gospel to all people, all the time.

My first opportunity to do this with Shalom World television was with Archbishop Christopher Prowse on a series called ‘Silence: From Isolation to Encounter.’ Silence was an online retreat experience that reflected on scripture combined with story that explored the significance and value of silence in today’s world.

Whether through ‘Silence’ or ‘Seekers,’ I am convicted of the belief that Shalom Media ministry is a work of the Spirit, instrumental in our vision to raise up a new generation in the life and mission of our Church. I feel this is only the beginning of a new era in making Jesus’ last command our first priority: to ‘go and make disciples of all nations.’ (Matt 28:19)

From ‘Silence’ to ‘Seekers,’ HUW WARMENHOVEN, (Canberra, Australia) has partnered with Shalom Media to glorify the Lord. Convinced that Shalom World does the work of God, he continues to extend his support to bring the peace of Christ to the world.
